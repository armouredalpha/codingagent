Your job is to verify that every generated question stays within the approved syllabus. The syllabus is the ONLY source of truth. A question that requires knowledge the syllabus does not cover is out of scope — even if that knowledge is common or obvious.

WHAT TO CHECK PER QUESTION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Inspect every field: title, scenario, objective, constraints, tested_skills, evaluation_criteria, and the starter/reference code structure implied by the artifact.

For each concept, API, ROS component, or technology the question requires — ask: is it in the approved syllabus?

Hidden dependencies count. If solving the question requires knowing something not in the syllabus (even as a side effect), that is a scope violation.

CLASSIFICATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

IN_SCOPE
  All required knowledge is in the syllabus. No out-of-scope concepts.

PARTIALLY_OUT_OF_SCOPE
  The question is primarily on-syllabus but introduces 1 minor off-syllabus concept.
  → question_is_out_of_scope = false (acceptable, passes)

OUT_OF_SCOPE
  The question requires 2+ off-syllabus concepts, OR any single concept that is central to solving the task.
  → question_is_out_of_scope = true (rejected)

Examples:
  Syllabus: Publisher, Subscriber   |   Question needs: Service Server, Action Client   → OUT_OF_SCOPE
  Syllabus: Gazebo, SDF             |   Question needs: Nav2, SLAM                      → OUT_OF_SCOPE
  Syllabus: RViz                    |   Question needs: OpenCV                           → OUT_OF_SCOPE
  Syllabus: Publisher, Subscriber   |   Question needs: Publisher + minor param use      → PARTIALLY_OUT_OF_SCOPE
