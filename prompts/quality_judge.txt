1. realism_score (0–100)
   Does this question resemble a real industrial robotics task?

   REWARD:
   - Named robot or industrial domain (warehouse, factory, mobile platform)
   - Concrete ROS interfaces: real topic names (/cmd_vel, /scan), message types (Twist, LaserScan), service names
   - Measurable success criteria tied to ROS behaviour (publish rate, service response content, node connectivity)
   - Failure/problem framing ("the conveyor stopped reporting", "the robot ignores stop signals")

   PENALISE:
   - Toy names: foo_node, /test_topic, example_pkg, hello_world
   - Vague objectives: "implement ROS communication", "create a working node"
   - Missing ROS interface definitions (no topic, no message type, no service)
   - Trivial single-call tasks a beginner could complete in under 5 minutes

   Scale anchor: 90+ = I would see this on a real job board. 50 = textbook exercise. <30 = toy.

2. hiring_signal_score (0–100)
   How much does a correct solution reveal about the candidate's engineering skill?

   REWARD:
   - Integration of 2+ ROS2 concepts that must work together
   - Debugging or fixing broken behaviour (not just implementing from scratch)
   - Design or architectural decision (choosing the right message type, timer frequency, QoS)
   - Real-world constraints (rate limits, error handling, state management)

   PENALISE:
   - Single-API call with no integration (just `create_publisher` and `publish`)
   - Solutions where copying the rclpy documentation tutorial would pass
   - No observable differentiation between a junior and a senior candidate

   Scale anchor: 90+ = strong hiring signal. 50 = basic competence check. <30 = anyone passes.

3. market_readiness
   What seniority level does a candidate who consistently solves questions like this demonstrate?
   Choose EXACTLY ONE: "Beginner" | "Employable" | "Job Ready" | "Industry Ready" | "Advanced Industry Ready"

   Beginner             → Can follow a tutorial. No real integration.
   Employable           → Can implement single ROS2 concepts correctly under guidance.
   Job Ready            → Can implement and connect ROS2 components without hand-holding.
   Industry Ready       → Can debug, design, and integrate multi-concept ROS2 systems.
   Advanced Industry Ready → Can architect end-to-end ROS2 workflows with edge cases.
